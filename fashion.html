Fashion Designing





Welcom to the Career Guide
for fashion Designing







A career in fashion is referred to as fashion designing which is the art of inventing/innovating or adding beauty to clothing by using certain accessories. The job of a fashion designer includes sketching fashion ideas, cutting out and sewing the clothing item, attending fashion shows, using tailoring skills to perfect the fit, drawing patterns to create garments, selecting fabrics and any special additions for the outfit and reading magazines to keep up with the latest fashions. Hence:



"A strong business sense plays an important role in the successful career of a
fashion designer."





Eligibility to become Fashion Designer


To be eligible for admission in fashion designing, candidate must clear 10+2 or its equivalent from a recognised school board in India. After the completion class 12, candidates can prepare for the entrance examinations like NIFT, NID. Candidates can also appear in CEED, UCEED, AIEED and other entrances for admission in UG & PG design courses.





Types of Job Roles Fashion Designer


The conceptualisation and development of intricate design details of a fabric are the work areas of a fashion designer. There are various job profiles for fashion designers after pursuing a fashion designing course, however, taking a job will depend on your interests and skills. Following are some of the job profiles for fashion designers that one can opt for:



Retail Manager:

A retail manager is responsible for ensuring that promotions and marketing gimmicks work smoothly, sets daily targets for the staff, and ensures customer satisfaction etc. He/she also heads the human resources and finance department in smaller enterprises.



Fashion Coordinators:

Fashion coordinators look after the advertising and promotions of the brand. They work closely with fabric dealers to bring out the best of products.



Fashion Stylist:

A stylist isresponsible for choosing the entire wardrobe of a fashion show. His/her job is to pick the right pieces from anyone’s wardrobe or from a designer’s collection, which would suit the client’s character, body type, the kind of occasion the dress is getting selected for.



Fashion Consultant:

As a fashion consultant, one has to keep a close eye on the latest trends of the market. The role of a fashion consultant also requires individuals to be keen observers of the changing fashion trends.



Textile Designer:

Textile designers work both independently or as part of a design team in both indusrial and non industrial settings. They are responsible for designing 2D patterns, both repititive and unique for knit patterns, weave and printed fabrics etc.



Make Up Artist:

Make up artists are hired for fashion events, weddings, pre-wedding photoshoots, bridal makeup, for models and celebrities. These artists are different than beauticians, as they do not take up normal beauty treatment jobs.



Sketching Assistant:

He/she is responsible for making technical sketches of garments for various presentations. These sketches are used as specifications on the designing and fabrication of the garments.



Fashion Journalist:

The fashion journalist has a job to write or report about latest fashion trends for a magazine. To be a fashion journalist one should to know the latest fashion trends and be in touch with the news of the fashion world.

Pros of becoming a Fashion Designer


The work of fashion designer is very creative and fun provided you have an eye for fashion. It all about coming up with new innovations which are trending or could trend in the future among people. The profession of fashion designing involves self satisfaction. A fashion designer comes up with new and improved trends to influence people to start using that particular clothing or accessory. So, if the product is used by large number of people, then the fashion designer will have the utmost satisfaction knowing that people are using something that has been his/her innovation. A career in fashion designing can prove very promising for those who love to travel. A career in fashion designing offers a lot of opportunities of traveling abroad if the clients are residing over there.





Cons of becoming a Fashion Designer


There are no fixed working hours for fashion designer. Usually the working hours of a fashion designer is long in order to meet deadlines and order supplies. The fashion industry is competitive and exhaustive.The stress that the individual has to encounter in this field can come in a number of different forms and if the person is unable to tackle stress, then, it can cause a disrupt and an eventual loss in the fashion business. The income or the pay scale of a fashion designer is hard to predict. A person who wishes to practice the art of fashion may not have a fixed financial stability. So it can be said that the income of a fashion designer is unpredictable.
